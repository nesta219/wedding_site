How We Met

16 December 2014

Mike first spotted Dana (and her red hair) at the NHL Holiday Party at Del Friscos in New York, but it wasn’t until the after party (and several whiskeys later) where he worked up enough confidence to finally talk to her! As soon as they met though, there was an instant connection and it has been history since then.

Our First Date

19 December 2014

Mike and Dana’s first date was in Manhattan's Lower East Side. They anxiously hopped around to different bars and restaurants in the area, eager to get to know each other better. They were both very excited about their newfound love interests.

The Proposal

16 December 2016

On the eve of the two-year anniversary of Dana & Michael's first meeting, they had planned for a night out and about in New York City to some of their favorite spots. On that list was a trip to the ice rink at Bryant Park, but little did Dana know that Mike had more planned than just skating! After a couple of laps, Mike pulled Dana into the center of the ice and prepared to get down on one knee, but Dana was so ecstatic she shouted “yes!” before he even touched the ice! It was a most romantic and thoughtful proposal, and the perfect place for these two-ice hockey-love birds!

Explore Lancaster

Our favorite Amish farm stand in the area, Eli Stoltzfus has a little bit of everything here. Amish wood works, crafts, quilts, baked goods, and of course their delicious Dutch Country Soft Pretzels!
You think you've played Mini Golf until you've played at the Village Greens in Strasburg, PA! There are two courses at the Village Greens and they are both unbelievably beautiful and unique! We personally like the Gold course (23 holes) for its challenging holes and beautiful landscaping, but you also can't go wrong with the Orange course (18 holes).
A McNamara family tradition, the Bird in Hand Family Inn was a great family vacation spot for Dana growing up. The Restaurant and Smorgasbord on site is not to be missed - excellent Dutch Country Cooking and friendly service! Try the shoo-fly- pie and red velvet for dessert!
A one-of- a-kind furniture shop, the Treasure Place has a great selection of refurbished furniture and home décor! It's fun to check out the new and inspiring pieces and also great to grab a beverage from the Corner Coffee shop here as well!
To really explore the area, try out a scooter tour! Scooting through the farmlands was a thrilling adventure and well worth it to get a different view of the farmland life!
One of our more adventurous endeavors here in Lancaster, the Hot Air Balloon Ride was an experience of a lifetime! With all the vast farmlands and beautiful scenery it was amazing to "float" through the air! We even almost landed in a corn field! Would highly recommend if you have the time!
